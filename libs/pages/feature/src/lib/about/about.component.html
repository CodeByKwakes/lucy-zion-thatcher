Lucy Thatcher is a writer, speaker and a sexual health adviser for the NHS. She has worked in sexual health clinics for over 15 years, across 5 London Boroughs. She has extensive knowledge of working with young people, victims of sexual assault, domestic violence and sexual exploitation and those from the LGBTQ+ community.

Lucy works to identify and fill the gaps in people’s sexual health education to empower them to make better decisions about their sexual health and relationships. She believes good self-esteem and education is the foundation of healthy choices and so she aims to reduce the shame and critical views people have of themselves and others by providing a safe and non-judgemental space to speak openly about sexual experiences and concerns, whilst providing accurate information and gently challenging people to consider a more compassionate position on some of their more judgemental and stigmatising points of view.

Lucy’s passion for sexual health has developed in tandem with her love of poetry. In 2017 she shone as a performance poet taking on projects exploring maternal mental health as well as black women in politics. In January 2020 she released her first book ‘Small Thoughts and Poetry’ quickly followed by ‘Naked’ in 2022 which explores themes such as unhealthy relationships and body acceptance through poetry and photography.

Lucy brings her full authenticity to the work she does by drawing on personal experience, her cultural upbringing and her creativity to adapt and appeal to a range of audiences through a variety of mediums.

The benefits of a healthy sex life are numerous. They include better sleep, less stress, decreased depression and anxiety as well as improved immune systems and self-esteem. So, in short healthier sex lives lead to happier people. My aim is to lift the shame of talking about sex in families and in relationships to encourage people to know, understand and clearly communicate their sexual boundaries, have a good knowledge of consent and how to establish it and how to negotiate the type of sex they like so we can all have a healthier and more pleasurable sex life.

– Lucy Zion Thatcher
